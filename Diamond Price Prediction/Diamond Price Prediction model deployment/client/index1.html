Welcome to Diamond Price Estimation

Diamonds are one of the most sought-after gemstones, valued for their beauty, rarity, and durability. However, their prices vary significantly based on several key characteristics. To accurately estimate a diamond’s price, it is essential to understand the factors that influence its value.

The most widely used classification system is based on the Four Cs—Carat, Cut, Color, and Clarity—which determine a diamond’s appearance and desirability. In addition to these, physical measurements like depth, table percentage, length, width, and height also play a crucial role in pricing.

Diamond Characteristics

  • Carat: Represents the weight of the diamond, which is one of the most significant factors influencing its price. One carat is equal to 1/5 g.
  • Cut: Indicates the quality of the diamond’s cut (e.g., Fair, Good, Very Good, Premium, Ideal). The cut affects the diamond's brilliance and is an important qualitative measure.
  • Color: Ranks the diamond's color from D (best, colorless) to J (poorest, faint yellow).
  • Clarity: Describes the diamond's internal and external inclusions, rated from I3 (lowest) to FL (highest, flawless).
  • Depth: Represents the height of the diamond as a percentage of its width.
  • Table: Represents the width of the diamond's top relative to its widest point (percentage).
  • Price: Represents the price of the diamond in US dollars, which serves as the response variable in the analysis.
  • x, y, z: Represents the diamond's length, width, and depth dimensions, respectively, in millimeters.

Diamond Cut Ranking

Order: Fair < Good < Very Good < Premium < Ideal

Cut Description
Fair Cut allows some light to escape from the bottom or sides of the diamond.
Good The diamond reflects more light than Fair but lacks optimal brilliance.
Very Good A well-cut diamond that offers excellent brilliance but may fall short of perfection.
Premium Near the top of the scale, with exceptional brilliance.
Ideal The cut maximizes light reflection and refraction, resulting in maximum brilliance.
